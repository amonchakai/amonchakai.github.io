Hg10 Policy

General information

Hg10 is an open-source XMPP client for BlackBerry 10. It enables you to connect to any server which use the XMPP protocol. This includes Google Hangout, Facebook messenger, Jabber, ...
The main focus of this application is Google Hangout, however Facebook messenger is also supported

Access to the source code:

The application is an open-source project under the GNU GPL licence. You can access to the code, edit it, modify it.

  • Souce code

Privacy:

Using this application, you can connect to your Google or Facebook account. These are sensitive data. This application will never share any private information. All your data always stay on your device. Your data will never be analysed, nor processed by this application. Everything stay on your phone. As previouly stated, the code of the application is open-source so you can check yourself what it does. No connection will never be made between your different accounts (Dropbox, Facebook, Google).

Permission:

This application deal with differnt kind of permissions:

Permission asked on your BlackBerry device

The application will request several permissions

  • Access to your files is only for uploading your images, documents, ... to dropbox and share the url to your contact. Rejecting it will only affect the file attachment feature.
  • Headless: Headless permission is required for this application: the core application is in the headless process. Rejecting it would result in a non-working application

Permission asked for Google Hangout

The access to your gmail account can be provided into the settings. This is not mandatory and does not affect the instant messaging service. Access to email is ONLY meant to retrive instant message's history which are stored on your Google account. Only READ access is provided, NO modification can be done on your email account from the application. Google API do not provide full access to your emails. No data is analysed, parsed, ... No data is send to any third party. The data always STAY on your device.

Permission asked for Facebook

The access to your facebook account can be provided into the settings. This is not mandatory and does not affect the instant messaging service. Access to facebook is ONLY meant to retrive instant message's history which are stored on your Facebook account. The application will only READ your inbox which contains the history of previous conversation. No data is analysed, parsed, ... No data is send to any third party. The data always STAY on your device.

Permission asked for dropbox

The access to your dropbox account can be provided into the settings. This is not mandatory and does not affect the instant messaging service. Access to dropbox is ONLY meant to host your files, images, ... on YOUR dropbox account into a restricted folder on your dropbox account. The application CANNOT go beyond its folder. Files stored in this folder is only accessible by you, and in case you share a file, the persons who recive the link YOU provide.
